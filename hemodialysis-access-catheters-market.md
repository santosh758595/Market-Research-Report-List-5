<p><p>Hemodialysis Access Catheters are used for vascular access during hemodialysis treatment. Short-term catheters are temporary access devices inserted into a large vein for short durations, primarily in emergency situations. Long-term catheters, on the other hand, are surgically implanted and remain in place for an extended period, providing continuous access for regular dialysis treatments. Both types of catheters play a vital role in ensuring effective hemodialysis treatment for patients with kidney failure.</p></p>
