<p><p>A GNSS Inertial Navigation System (INS) is a navigation system that combines the Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) with an inertial navigation system to provide accurate positioning and orientation data. The market growth is driven by the increasing demand for precise navigation systems in various industries such as aerospace, defense, marine, and automotive. The rise in the adoption of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) and autonomous vehicles is also boosting the demand for GNSS INS systems.</p><p>One of the key trends in the GNSS INS market is the development of integrated navigation systems that combine multiple sensors such as cameras, LiDAR, and radar to enhance the accuracy and reliability of navigation. Additionally, advancements in MEMS (micro-electro-mechanical systems) technology are leading to the miniaturization and cost reduction of GNSS INS systems, further driving market growth.</p></p>
