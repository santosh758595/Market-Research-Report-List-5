<p><p>Physician-prescribed oral appliances market encompasses two main types: Mandibular Advancement Devices (MADs) and Tongue-retaining Devices. MADs are designed to protrude the lower jaw forward, thereby preventing the collapse of soft tissues in the throat that can obstruct breathing during sleep. Tongue-retaining Devices work by holding the tongue in a forward position to keep the airway open. Both types of oral appliances are commonly used in the treatment of obstructive sleep apnea and snoring, providing patients with a non-invasive alternative to Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP) therapy.</p></p>
