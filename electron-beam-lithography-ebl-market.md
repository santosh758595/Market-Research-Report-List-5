<p><p>Electron Beam Lithography (EBL) is a technique used for creating extremely fine patterns on surfaces using a focused beam of electrons. There are two main types of sources used in EBL machines: Thermionic Sources and Field Electron Emission Sources. Thermionic Sources rely on heating a filament to release electrons, while Field Electron Emission Sources generate electrons through the application of a strong electric field. Both types of sources play a crucial role in enabling precise and high-resolution lithography in various industries.</p></p>
