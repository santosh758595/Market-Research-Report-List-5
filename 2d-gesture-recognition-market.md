<p><p>2D Gesture Recognition includes Static Gesture Recognition and Dynamic Gesture Recognition. In Static Gesture Recognition, the system recognizes hand or body movements in a fixed position without changes over time. This type is commonly used in touchless interfaces and control systems. On the other hand, Dynamic Gesture Recognition involves tracking and interpreting gestures as they evolve over time, allowing for more complex interactions like sign language recognition or virtual reality control. Both types play a crucial role in advancing human-computer interaction and user experience technologies.</p></p>
