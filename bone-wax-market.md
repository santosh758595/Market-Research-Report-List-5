Absorbable bone wax dissolves over time, allowing for natural healing of the bone. On the other hand, non-absorbable bone wax remains in place permanently to provide long-term hemostasis. Surgeons choose between these two types based on the specific needs of the patient and the surgical procedure being performed.</p></p>
