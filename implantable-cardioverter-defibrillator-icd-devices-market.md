<p><p>Implantable Cardioverter Defibrillator (ICD) devices are electronic devices placed under the skin to help control irregular heart rhythms. There are two main types of ICD devices: single-chamber ICD and dual-chamber ICD. Single-chamber ICDs have one lead placed in either the atrium or ventricle of the heart, while dual-chamber ICDs have leads in both the atrium and ventricle. Dual-chamber ICDs are able to sense and treat abnormal heart rhythms more effectively, providing more comprehensive monitoring and treatment options for patients.</p></p>
