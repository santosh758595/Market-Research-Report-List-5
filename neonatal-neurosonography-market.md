<p><p>Neonatal neurosonography refers to the use of ultrasound imaging to assess the brain and nervous system of newborns. The market for neonatal neurosonography includes different types of technology, such as 2D, 3D, 4D, and Doppler. </p><p>2D imaging provides a two-dimensional view of the brain and helps in detecting structural abnormalities. </p><p>3D imaging offers a three-dimensional representation of the brain, providing more detailed information for a more accurate diagnosis. </p><p>4D imaging adds the element of motion to the images, allowing for a better understanding of the brain's functionality. </p><p>Doppler imaging measures blood flow in the brain, helping to assess vascular health and detect any abnormalities. Each of these technologies plays a crucial role in diagnosing and monitoring neurological conditions in newborns.</p></p>
