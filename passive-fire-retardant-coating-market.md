<p><p>Passive fire retardant coatings can be classified into two main types - intumescent and cementitious. Intumescent coatings expand when exposed to high temperatures, forming a protective char layer that insulates the substrate from fire. Cementitious coatings are cement-based and provide fire resistance through their ability to withstand extreme heat. These coatings can further be categorized based on the technology used, such as water-based or solvent-based coatings, which determine the application method and environmental impact of the coating.</p></p>
