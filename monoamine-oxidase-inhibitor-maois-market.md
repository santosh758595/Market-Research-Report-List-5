Nonselective MAO-A/MAO-B inhibitors target both enzyme types, while selective MAO-A/MAO-B inhibitors target only one specific enzyme. The market for nonselective MAOIs tends to be larger, as they have a broader effect on neurotransmitters, while selective MAOIs may have a more targeted approach and potentially fewer side effects. Both types of MAOIs play a crucial role in the treatment of depression.</p></p>
