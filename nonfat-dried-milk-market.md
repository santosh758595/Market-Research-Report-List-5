<p><p>Nonfat dried milk is classified into three categories based on the heat treatment it undergoes during production: low-heat, medium-heat, and high-heat. Low-heat nonfat dried milk is processed at the lowest temperatures, preserving the most nutrients and flavor. Medium-heat dried milk is processed at slightly higher temperatures, resulting in some nutrient loss but still retaining good flavor. High-heat dried milk is processed at the highest temperatures, which may cause a significant loss of nutrients but extends its shelf life. Each type caters to different consumer preferences and desired end uses.</p></p>
