<p><p>Cardiovascular pericardial patches are used in surgical procedures to repair damaged or weakened parts of the heart. There are three main types of pericardial patches: autologous, xenograft, and synthetic. Autologous patches are made from the patient's own tissue, xenograft patches are derived from animal tissue, and synthetic patches are made from artificial materials. Each type of patch has its own unique benefits and drawbacks, and the choice of patch type depends on the specific needs of the patient and the surgical procedure.</p></p>
