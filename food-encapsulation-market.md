<p><p>Food encapsulation is a process where active ingredients are surrounded by a coating material to protect against degradation during storage or processing. The market includes three main types: microencapsulation, which involves particles ranging from 1 to 1000 µm in size; nanoencapsulation, which uses particles smaller than 100nm for better bioavailability; and hybrid encapsulation, which combines techniques for improved performance. Each type offers unique benefits in terms of stability, controlled release, and targeted delivery of functional ingredients in food products.</p></p>
