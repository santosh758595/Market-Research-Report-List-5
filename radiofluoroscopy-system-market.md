<p><p>Radiofluoroscopy systems are classified based on their field strength into Low Field, Midfield, High Field, and Ultra-High Field categories. Low Field systems are suitable for basic imaging with low resolution. Midfield systems offer better image quality and are often used in diagnostic imaging. High Field systems provide high-quality images for advanced diagnostic procedures. Ultra-High Field systems are the most advanced, offering superior image resolution and clarity, making them ideal for research and specialized clinical applications.</p></p>
